Equipping the right Biosuit is critical to surviving the harsh sci-fi battlefields of Netmarble's cross-platform MMORPG, and the **rf online next arbiter** stands out as one of the most flexible combat chassis available. Offering a balanced mix of close-range melee strikes and mid-range tactical pressure, this hybrid class requires players to master positioning and flight mechanics to unleash its full potential.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Arbiter Skills, Customizations, and Add-ons">

The Arbiter relies on a suite of physical strikes and energy-imbued abilities. Growth in RF Online Next is governed by several core layers: Adding Skills, Skill Enhancements, Add-Ons, and Finishing Skills. For the Arbiter, customizations should focus on keeping your target controlled while maximizing your physical DPS.

Skills are adjusted via the in-game skills menu, where you can slot specific Add-Ons based on the encounter type. Because you are dealing with a hybrid range, selecting add-ons that offer crowd control (like slows and stuns) or mobility enhancements is essential.

| Skill Category | Recommended Add-On | Target Use Case | Combat Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Offensive Strikes** | Damage Uptime | Farming & Dungeon Waves | Multiplies physical hit scaling |
| **Control Abilities** | Cooldown Reduction | PvP Skirmishes | Rapid crowd-control execution |
| **Defensive Buffs** | Evasion Boost | Escape & Relocation | Mitigates damage during retreat |

<Callout type="tip" title="Skill Growth Strategy">
Avoid upgrading every single skill at once. Focus your resources on the core damage and control skills you actively cast during your main rotations to bypass resource bottlenecks.
</Callout>

Large-scale PvP in RF Online Next takes place on massive battlefields where faction honor and mining control are contested. The Mining War revolves around excavators that lose HP based on hit count rather than raw damage numbers. The faction that successfully destroys opposing excavators or maintains the highest remaining HP wins the war.

As an Arbiter player, you must use the environment and vertical space to ambush opponents. Use the airfield structures to take off, but keep an eye on your fuel reserves.
