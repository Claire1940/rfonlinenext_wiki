<Section icon={Swords} title="Optimizing Your Build: Skills, Gear, and Rovers">
Effective **RF Online Next progression** goes beyond just choosing a Biosuit. It involves strategic investment in gear enhancement, skill growth, collection stats, and Rover companions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose One Main Damage Biosuit First">
    Start by investing in the Biosuit you use for main quests, dungeons, and boss contributions. Firepower-oriented suits like **Demolisher** and **Punisher** are practical anchors for early solo progress. Treat other Biosuits as situational swap tools until your main route has stable gear, skills, and passive stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Use Swapping for Roles, Not Random Spending">
    Biosuit swapping allows you to change combat styles. Use a damage suit for farming and quest clearing, and keep a defensive or support option ready for PvP, party play, or challenging boss patterns. Avoid splitting upgrade materials evenly across every Biosuit early on.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Fix Gear Enhancement Before Chasing Side Systems">
    When progress slows, upgrade your equipped gear. Equipment Enhancement includes Enhance, Batch Enhance, Prime Mod, and Random Option layers. Early bottlenecks are often resolved faster by safely enhancing active equipment rather than gambling on a new setup.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Raise Skill Power on the Skills You Actually Cast">
    Prioritize the active skills used by your main Biosuit. Skill growth supports Adding Skills, Skill Enhancements, Add-Ons, and Finishing Skills. Add-ons should support your current objective, whether it's damage uptime for farming or control for PvP.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Complete Collection Stats with Attack and Accuracy First">
    Collection progress provides passive stat value. Attack increases damage directly, and accuracy ensures your hits land, making other damage upgrades worthwhile. After these, consider durability, evasion, or role-specific stats based on your needs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Use Rovers as Passive Growth, Not Decoration">
    Rovers are companion units that offer practical stat and utility bonuses. Officially surfaced Rover systems include Fuse, Collection, Finalize, Tuning, Rover Workshop, and Rover Burst. Align Rover growth with your main build for effects like EXP gain and attack-related stats.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success">
A balanced approach to skill and gear progression ensures sustained growth without hitting unnecessary roadblocks. Always prioritize upgrades that directly impact your primary combat effectiveness.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="PvP and Faction Warfare: Advanced Strategies">
**RF Online Next's** massive PvP and faction warfare are central to the game's identity. Successful engagement requires strategic positioning, Biosuit role swaps, and effective use of Sacred Weapons.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Mining War Objectives">
    - **Excavator HP** is reduced by hit count, not damage value.
    - Destroying all opposing excavators wins the war.
    - If excavators remain, the guild from the faction with the highest remaining HP wins.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Holy Stone Keeper Importance">
    - Spawns one minute after Faction War starts, respawns three minutes after defeat.
    - Defeating it can grant a **barrier to your faction's excavator**.
    - Final-hit faction receives escalating combat buffs (movement speed, attack, defense, accuracy, evasion).
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
Utilize the Mercenary System as a guild leader to set recruitment criteria and prize rewards, attracting skilled players to bolster your faction's forces during critical battles. Recruitment is available until 21:10 UTC, with prize ranges from 100 to 100,000 Diamonds.
</Callout>

### Key PvP Elements
- **Biosuit Swapping**: Change combat style (offense, evasion, support) to adapt to real-time battle situations.
- **Flight Mechanics**: Airfields open ten minutes into battle, allowing for strategic movement and flanking. Be mindful of fuel limits to avoid aerial pitfalls.
- **Battlefield Structures**: Turrets (activated by Holy Stone Keeper control) can destroy barricades, opening paths to enemy bases. Guardians defend entrances and excavators.
