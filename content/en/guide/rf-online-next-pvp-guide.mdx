<Section icon={Map} title="Faction Warfare & Mining War Objectives">

The core of the **rf online next pvp guide** revolves around large-scale faction warfare, where thousands of players clash to secure territorial dominance and valuable resources. The premier conflict on the schedule is the Mining War, a high-stakes battleground where the three primary factions compete over resource excavators. Understanding the unique mechanics of these excavators is the first step toward securing victory for your guild and faction.

Unlike standard PvE targets, resource excavators do not take damage based on your raw attack power. Instead, their HP is reduced strictly by the number of hits they receive. This mechanical quirk completely shifts the viability of certain builds, making high-attack-speed classes and multi-hit skills incredibly valuable during siege phases.

| Objective | Spawn Time | HP Mechanic | Victory Condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Excavators** | Active at start | **Hit count reduction** | Destroy all opposing excavators |
| **Holy Stone Keeper** | 1 min after start | Standard damage | Land final hit for faction buff |
| **Airfield Paths** | 10 mins after start | N/A | Connects faction airfields |

The battlefield is further complicated by verticality. Exactly ten minutes after the Mining War begins, the airfield opens, allowing players to fly directly between the Accretia, Bellato, and Cora airfields. This introduces flanking routes and aerial combat opportunities that can bypass ground-level chokepoints entirely.

<Callout type="warning" title="Flight Fuel Warning">

Running out of fuel mid-flight results in an immediate fuel shortage state, causing your character to fall to their death. Always plan your flight paths with fuel efficiency in mind.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Biosuit Swapping Strategies">

One of the defining features of combat is the ability to swap Biosuits dynamically on the battlefield. Rather than being locked into a single class, you can switch your active suit to adapt to the changing flow of a team fight. This flexibility is a core pillar of any advanced **rf online next pvp guide**.

In large-scale PvP, S-Tier suits like the Demolisher and Punisher are excellent for initial engagements due to their long-range physical AoE and stable ranged damage. However, if the enemy vanguard successfully closes the gap with MAUs, players must quickly transition to defensive or crowd-control suits to survive.

| Biosuit | PvP Tier | Role | Key Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Demolisher** | **S-Tier** | Long-range Physical AoE | Clustered target devastation |
| **Punisher** | **S-Tier** | Ranged Fire Support | Stable damage and ally cover |
| **Enforcer** | **A-Tier** | Frontline Guardian | High durability and taunts |
| **Psypher** | **A-Tier** | Battlefield Control | Force-based formation disruption |
| **Phantom** | **B-Tier** | High-speed Assassin | Backline priority target picks |

Dynamic swapping allows a coordinated squad to cycle their roles. For example, when pushing an excavator, a squad might start with five Demolishers to clear out defenders, swap to Enforcers to absorb turret fire, and then switch to Technicians to deploy drones and heal the frontline.

<Callout type="info" title="Resource Management">
Avoid splitting your upgrade resources evenly across every Biosuit early on. Budget your upgrade materials to maximize the gear, skills, and passive stats of one primary damage suit first. Treat your secondary suits as situational utility swaps until your main build is fully optimized.
</Callout>
