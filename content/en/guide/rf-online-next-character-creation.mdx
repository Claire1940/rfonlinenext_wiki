<Section icon={Swords} title="Early Progression: From Account Setup to Level 30">
The first few hours in RF Online Next are designed to introduce you to the core mechanics and guide you through your initial progression. The game tracks milestones through achievements, providing a clear roadmap for new players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Install from an Official Platform">
    Ensure you download RF Online Next from the official Netmarble site, Epic Games Store, Google Play, or App Store. This guarantees you have the legitimate game client and helps avoid unofficial APKs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Create Your Game Account and Character">
    After logging in, choose your desired region and server. This choice is critical as it determines your gameplay environment and whom you'll be able to interact with.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Set Up PC Controls">
    For PC players, familiarize yourself with the default controls: W, A, S, D for movement and Space Bar for flight. Adjust graphics settings in the Environment tab for optimal performance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reach Level 5 and Complete Tutorials">
    Focus on clearing early quests to hit level 5, unlocking achievements like "The Great Beginning." Complete the Biosuit Flight Tutorial ("Colleague in Danger Quest") and the Biosuit Equipping Tutorial ("Bellato's Ammunition Depot Quest") to learn essential mechanics.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Clear Ancient Ruins and Deploy Sacred Weapons">
    Defeat the Holy Stone Keeper in the Arcane Ruins Quest, a key early boss encounter. Practice deploying Sacred Weapons (Launcher, MAU, Animus) five times to earn the "Steely Watcher" achievement, preparing you for large-scale combat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Push to Level 30 and Regional Main Quests">
    Continue following main quests to reach level 30, earning the "Valiant Mercenary" achievement. Progress through regional main quests in Dorian, Numerus, and Kallis to further unlock content and story elements.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Biosuit Roles and Beginner-Friendly Options">
Choosing the right Biosuit for your initial playstyle can greatly influence your early game comfort. While all Biosuits are viable, some are more beginner-friendly due to their straightforward mechanics or durability. Here's a quick overview of some Biosuits and their suitability for new players.

| Biosuit Name | Role | Range | Beginner Fit | PvE Use | PvP Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Demolisher** | Mid-to-long-range physical AoE damage | Mid to Long | Very High | Mob clearing, farming, dungeon waves | Area pressure, backline fire support |
| **Punisher** | Stable ranged damage and ally cover | Ranged | High | Safe questing, consistent attacks | Cover fire, crowd control pressure |
| **Enforcer** | Frontline guardian and tank | Close | High (for durable play) | Survival, multi-enemy pulls, safer boss learning | Shielding, taunt pressure, frontline disruption |
| **Dreadnought** | Charging melee destroyer | Close | Medium-High | Melee questing, direct burst | Chasing targets, combo pressure |
| **Psypher** | Force-based battlefield control | Mid | Medium | Grouped enemy control, tactical questing | Formation disruption, crowd control |
| **Technician** | Support, healing, drones, towers | Ranged Support | Medium (for party play) | Party support, healing, utility | Sustain support, defensive utility |
| **Phantom** | High-speed assassin | Close | Advanced | Fast single-target eliminations | Backline picks, high-risk engages |
| **Arbiter** | Close-to-mid-range hybrid combat | Close to Mid | Medium | Hybrid damage routes, flexible questing | Pressure tools, flexible skirmishing |

For most new players, Biosuits like **Demolisher** and **Punisher** offer a more forgiving entry into RF Online Next due to their ranged combat and consistent damage. Enforcer is also a strong choice for those who prefer durability.

<Callout type="warning" title="Advanced Biosuits">
Phantom, while powerful, is an advanced Biosuit that punishes poor timing and positioning. It is recommended for players who have a strong grasp of the game's mechanics and enemy patterns.
</Callout>
