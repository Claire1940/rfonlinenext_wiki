<Section icon={Swords} title="Guild Wars and Mining War Objectives">

The ultimate test for any alliance is the Faction War, commonly known as the Mining War. This massive PvP event places thousands of players on a single battlefield where coordinated guild actions determine the winning faction. The primary objective during these wars is to secure and defend the faction's Excavators. Unlike standard targets, Excavators do not take damage based on raw DPS. Instead, their HP is reduced by the total number of landing hits, making fast-attacking Biosuits highly valuable for objective focus.

Ten minutes after the Faction War begins, the airfield opens, connecting the bases of Accretia, Bellato, and Cora. This introduces verticality and flanking opportunities. Guilds must position aerial squads to control these flight corridors. However, players must monitor their fuel levels carefully; flying too high or staying airborne for too long leads to fuel exhaustion and rapid defeat.

| Objective | Spawn Time | Mechanics | Reward / Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Airfield** | 10 mins post-start | Connects faction bases | Allows flanking routes |
| **Holy Stone Keeper** | 1 min post-start | Respawns 3 mins after defeat | Excavator barrier & buffs |
| **Excavator** | Active at start | HP reduced by hit count | Decides winning faction |

The Holy Stone Keeper is another critical objective that spawns in the center of the battlefield one minute after the war starts. If your faction lands the final hit on this boss, your active Excavators receive a protective barrier. Additionally, your entire faction gains an escalating combat buff that improves movement speed, physical attack, defense, accuracy, and evasion. 

<Callout type="warning" title="Objective Priority">
Never abandon your faction's Excavators to chase individual kills. A single coordinated enemy squad can destroy an unprotected Excavator in seconds due to the hit-count damage mechanic.
</Callout>

If any Excavators remain standing when the war timer expires, victory is awarded to the faction with the highest remaining Excavator HP. Guild leaders must designate specific squads for defensive guard duty near base entrances while high-mobility strike teams push toward enemy Excavators.

<Section icon={Target} title="Mercenary System and Recruitment Rules">

To bolster their ranks during massive conflicts, guild leaders can utilize the robust Mercenary System. This feature allows organized guilds to temporarily hire skilled solo players to fight under their banner during Faction Wars. It provides solo players with a lucrative source of income while giving competitive guilds the raw numbers needed to overwhelm opposing factions.

The recruitment process is highly structured and governed by strict server-wide deadlines. Guild Masters must set their recruitment criteria and allocate the prize pool well in advance of the battle. The system operates on a bidding and application process that closes automatically as the war preparation phase transitions into active combat.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set Recruitment Criteria">
    Guild Masters define the minimum level requirements and preferred Biosuit classes in the Mercenary Registry menu.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Fund the Prize Pool">
    Allocate a Diamond reward pool from the guild treasury. The permissible prize range is strictly set between 100 and 100,000 Diamonds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Lock Recruitment Settings">
    All recruitment listings must be finalized before the 21:10 server deadline. No new mercenary slots can be created after this point.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize Prize Allocations">
    The Diamond prize amounts become completely locked at 21:25. Ensure your allocations are correct before this final cutoff.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Treasury Management">
Budget your guild's Diamond reserves carefully. Offering higher prize pools naturally attracts top-tier solo players, but overspending on minor skirmishes can leave your treasury depleted for major seasonal campaigns.
</Callout>

Once hired, mercenaries receive real-time map indicators and objective directives set by the hiring guild's leadership. When the war concludes, the system automatically distributes the allocated Diamonds to the mercenaries based on their participation and contribution metrics, ensuring fair payment without manual intervention.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Guild Auctions and World Boss Contribution">

End-game progression in RF Online Next relies heavily on defeating World Bosses and clearing high-tier Zone Dungeons. These encounters yield rare crafting materials, Epic Rover summon chests, and Sacred Weapon upgrade kits. However, securing these rewards requires a high contribution score, which can be achieved through individual, party, or guild-wide performance during the raid.

When a World Boss is defeated, the loot is not simply distributed randomly. Instead, valuable items are placed into the Guild Auction system. This system allows active members to bid on rare drops using Diamonds. Once the auction concludes, the spent Diamonds are distributed as dividends to all guild members who participated in the raid, rewarding everyone's effort.
